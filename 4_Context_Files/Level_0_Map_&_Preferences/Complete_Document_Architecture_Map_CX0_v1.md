# COMPLETE DOCUMENT ARCHITECTURE MAP
## From Foundation to Execution: The Complete System

---

## PURPOSE OF THIS MAP

This document shows the complete hierarchy of documentation needed to run a coaching/consulting business with courses, community, and content - from foundational identity documents down to individual content asset creation workflows.

Think of this as the "master blueprint" showing how every document type relates to every other document type, preventing duplication and ensuring systematic thinking at every level.

---

## THE ARCHITECTURE: 6 LEVELS

### Level 0: Foundation Documents (Personal & Business Identity)
WHO YOU ARE - The unchanging core that informs everything else

### Level 1: Strategic Documents (Business Direction)
WHERE YOU'RE GOING - Multi-year vision and positioning

### Level 2: Portfolio Documents (Offering Architecture)
WHAT YOU OFFER - How services and products fit together

### Level 3: Offering Specifications (Individual Product/Service Design)
HOW EACH OFFERING WORKS - Detailed blueprints

### Level 4: Content Strategy (Marketing & Delivery Assets)
WHAT CONTENT YOU NEED - The bridge to execution

### Level 5: Asset Production (Individual Deliverables)
ACTUAL EXECUTION - Checklists and workflows

## LEVEL 1: STRATEGIC DOCUMENTS

These define your multi-year direction and positioning. Updated quarterly or annually.

### Document 1.1: Business Strategy & Positioning

What it is: Your overall business vision, model, and strategic direction

What it contains:
- Vision for 1-3 years
- Business model (how you make money)
- Core positioning in market
- Target market definition
- Revenue goals and strategy
- How offerings fit together strategically
- Competitive differentiation
- Growth strategy

How it connects:
- UP to Level 0: Must align with personal mission and values
- DOWN to Level 2: Determines which offerings to create
- DOWN to Level 4: Shapes all content messaging

Update frequency: Annually, with quarterly reviews

### Document 1.2: Annual Operating Plan

What it is: This year's specific goals, priorities, and resource allocation

What it contains:
- This year's specific revenue goals
- Key initiatives and projects
- Resource allocation (time, money, attention)
- Quarterly milestones
- Success metrics
- What you're NOT doing this year

How it connects:
- UP to 1.1: Implements the multi-year strategy
- HORIZONTAL to SC_Layer 5: This feeds your Master Timeline
- DOWN to Level 2: Determines which offerings get built this year

Update frequency: Annually, with quarterly adjustments

### Document 1.3: Master Timeline & Resource Allocation

What it is: Your orchestration layer across ALL work (this is your SC_Layer 5)

What it contains:
- Available capacity assessment
- Active strategic projects
- 12-week rolling execution timeline
- Weekly execution detail
- Time allocation across all offerings/projects

How it connects:
- UP to 1.2: Executes the annual plan within real constraints
- DOWN to Level 3-5: Determines when each offering/content gets focus
- HORIZONTAL to SuperContext: This IS SC_Layer 5 of your SuperContext

Update frequency: Weekly for execution detail, monthly for rolling timeline

## LEVEL 2: PORTFOLIO DOCUMENTS

These show how all your offerings fit together as a coherent system. Updated quarterly.

### Document 2.1: Complete Offering Portfolio Map

What it is: The complete view of everything you offer and how it connects

What it contains:

#### Professional Services Section
- Coaching packages (what they are, who they're for, pricing)
- Consulting engagements (scope, deliverables, pricing)
- How services connect to each other
- Typical client journey through services

#### Products Section
- Courses (what exists, what's planned)
- Community (structure, benefits, pricing)
- Digital products (templates, toolkits, etc.)
- How products connect to each other
- How products lead to services (or vice versa)

#### Portfolio Architecture
- Entry points (how people discover you)
- Natural progressions (what leads to what)
- Pricing strategy across portfolio
- Revenue model (which offerings generate what % of revenue)

How it connects:
- UP to 1.1: Implements the business strategy
- DOWN to Level 3: Each offering gets its own specifications
- DOWN to Level 4: Portfolio determines overall content strategy

Update frequency: Quarterly, as you add/retire offerings

### Document 2.2: Client Journey Map (Cross-Portfolio)

What it is: How someone moves from stranger to advocate across all offerings

What it contains:
- Awareness stage (how they discover you)
- Consideration stage (how they evaluate you)
- Decision stage (how they choose to work with you)
- Experience stage (what happens when they buy)
- Advocacy stage (how they become promoters)
- Content/touchpoints at each stage
- Which offerings serve which stages

How it connects:
- UP to 2.1: Shows how portfolio offerings connect
- DOWN to Level 4: Determines what content is needed at each stage
- HORIZONTAL to Business Foundation: Maps to Client Journey Map document

Update frequency: Quarterly or when major offerings change

## LEVEL 3: OFFERING SPECIFICATIONS

Detailed blueprint for each individual offering. One document per offering.

### Template 3.1: Course/Product Specifications

What it is: Complete specifications for a single course or digital product

What it contains:

#### Strategic Positioning
- What problem it solves
- Who it's for (specific avatar)
- Transformation promise
- Unique value proposition
- Positioning vs. alternatives
- Pricing and justification

#### Product Structure
- Modules/sections overview
- Learning objectives per module
- Delivery method and platform
- Timeline/duration
- Prerequisites (if any)
- Completion criteria

#### Content Outline
- Detailed module breakdown
- Lesson topics within each module
- Exercise/worksheet descriptions
- Assessment approach
- Resource materials needed

#### Technical Specifications
- Platform/tools used
- File formats and sizes
- Access/login requirements
- Integration needs

#### Success Metrics
- Student outcomes to measure
- Completion rates
- Satisfaction targets
- Business impact (revenue, leads, positioning)

How it connects:
- UP to 2.1: This is one offering in the portfolio
- DOWN to Level 4: Specifications inform content strategy for this offering
- DOWN to Level 5: Specifications inform asset production

Update frequency: Only when product strategy changes

#### Deliverables

- Templates or tools provided

- Follow-up included

- Typical session progression
- Milestones and checkpoints

#### Success Criteria
- Client outcomes to achieve
- How success is measured
- Your capacity (how many clients)

How it connects:

## LEVEL 4: CONTENT STRATEGY DOCUMENTS

The bridge between offering specifications and actual content creation. One per offering (or one master with sections per offering).

### Template 4.1: Offering Content Strategy & Asset Map

What it is: The complete content plan for marketing and delivering a specific offering

What it contains:

#### Section 1: Content Pillars
- 3-5 core themes for this offering's content
- How pillars support positioning
- Examples of each pillar

#### Section 2: Customer Journey Content Map
For each journey stage, what content is needed:

##### Awareness Stage Content
- Social posts
- Blog articles
- Videos
- Podcast appearances
- SEO content
- Paid ad creative

##### Consideration Stage Content
- Lead magnet
- Email nurture sequence
- Webinar/workshop
- Case studies
- FAQ content
- Comparison content

##### Decision Stage Content
- Sales page/landing page
- Enrollment materials
- Pricing presentation
- Consultation scripts
- Proposal templates

##### Experience Stage Content
- Onboarding materials
- Course content/session materials
- Templates and tools
- Community engagement prompts
- Progress milestones
- Support resources

##### Advocacy Stage Content
- Testimonial requests
- Referral materials
- Advanced content for alumni
- Case study templates
- Community leadership opportunities

#### Section 3: Master Asset Inventory

Complete list of every content asset needed with:
- Asset name
- Type (blog, email, video, etc.)
- Purpose (which stage it serves)
- Status (not started / in progress / complete)
- Due date
- Owner
- Location/URL

Example format:
```
AWARENESS STAGE
[ ] Blog: "The 4 Levels of Navigation" | Due: Week 2 | Status: Complete | Location: blog.com/4-levels

CONSIDERATION STAGE  
[ ] Lead Magnet: "AI Mastery System" | Due: Week 1 | Status: Complete | Location: leadmagnet-url
[ ] Email Sequence: 7-day nurture | Due: Week 3 | Status: In Progress
```

#### Section 4: Messaging Templates

Standard ways to describe this offering in different contexts:

- Elevator pitch (30 seconds)
- One-paragraph description
- Email signature version
- Social media bio version
- Landing page headline
- Key benefits (3-5 bullets)
- Objection responses
- FAQ answers

#### Section 5: Content Calendar

- What gets created when
- Publication schedule
- Promotion schedule
- Seasonal/event-based content
- Evergreen vs. timely content

#### Section 6: Distribution Strategy

- Where content lives (blog, YouTube, LinkedIn, etc.)
- Cross-promotion approach
- Paid vs. organic
- Frequency by channel
- Repurposing strategy

#### Section 7: Content Creation Guidelines

- Voice and tone for this offering
- Visual standards (from Brand Guidelines)
- Quality bar
- Review/approval process
- Tools and templates to use

How it connects:
- UP to Level 3: Informed by offering specifications
- UP to Level 0: Governed by Brand Guidelines and Key Message Platform
- DOWN to Level 5: Determines which assets to produce
- HORIZONTAL across Level 4: Assets may serve multiple offerings

Update frequency: Monthly for asset inventory, quarterly for strategy

## LEVEL 5: ASSET PRODUCTION

The actual execution layer - checklists and workflows for creating individual content pieces.

### Category 5.1: Written Content Assets

#### Workflow 5.1.1: Blog Post Production

Checklist:
- [ ] Review Content Strategy for topic and messaging
- [ ] Review Brand Guidelines for voice
- [ ] Create outline using pillar framework
- [ ] Write draft
- [ ] Add SEO elements (keywords, meta, etc.)
- [ ] Add internal links
- [ ] Source/create images
- [ ] Review against quality standards
- [ ] Schedule publication
- [ ] Update Asset Inventory as complete
- [ ] Promote according to distribution strategy

Tools needed:
- Content Strategy document (reference)
- Brand Guidelines (reference)
- Blog platform access
- Image tools
- SEO tools

Time estimate: 2-3 hours

AI leverage points:
- Outline creation
- First draft
- SEO optimization
- Meta descriptions
- Social promotion posts

#### Workflow 5.1.2: Email Sequence Production

Checklist:
- [ ] Review Content Strategy for sequence purpose and messaging
- [ ] Map customer journey stage
- [ ] Define sequence goals (open rate, click rate, conversion)
- [ ] Outline email progression (what each email does)
- [ ] Write email 1 - subject line, body, CTA
- [ ] Write email 2 - subject line, body, CTA
- [ ] [Continue for all emails in sequence]
- [ ] Review against Brand Guidelines voice
- [ ] Set up in email platform
- [ ] Configure automation triggers
- [ ] Test sequence
- [ ] Activate
- [ ] Update Asset Inventory as complete

Tools needed:
- Content Strategy document
- Email platform (ConvertKit, etc.)
- Brand Guidelines

Time estimate: 4-6 hours for 5-7 email sequence

AI leverage points:
- Email drafts
- Subject line variations
- Personalization elements
- A/B testing ideas

#### Workflow 5.1.3: Landing Page Copy Production

- [ ] Update Asset Inventory as complete

Tools needed:

## THE KEY INSIGHT

This architecture prevents:
- Creating content without strategic foundation
- Building offerings without understanding portfolio fit
- Executing without clear specifications
- Reinventing process for every asset

This architecture enables:
- Systematic thinking at every level
- Clear dependencies and relationships
- Knowing exactly what document answers what question
- Leveraging AI effectively because context is always clear

You don't need to create all of this at once. Build Level 0 and 1 thoroughly, then build Level 2-5 only for offerings you're actively launching.

But having the architecture clarifies what you need and prevents duplication or gaps.

## 1. Offering Context Summary

**Transformation promise:** Proof of methodology value at low risk — master one specific framework application in a short, affordable course before committing to the full Foundations curriculum.
**Primary audience segment:** ICP-Course (and ICP-Community for upgrading members) — qualified prospects not yet ready for $2K+ investment; budget-conscious but methodology-curious; community members wanting structured first step.
**Primary journey stages served:** Stage 2 (Consideration) → Stage 3 (Decision — direct purchase trigger)
**Content goal:** Content-to-email capture-to-purchase. At $97–297, the conversion mechanism is email marketing + search discovery. No discovery call required. YouTube drives search discovery; email closes the sale.

**Best practices basis:** Entry-level courses ($97–297) convert primarily through email (warmest audience converts highest), YouTube (searchable authority content with email list CTA), and free workshop/lead magnet as conversion trigger. Launch email sequence: 8–12 well-crafted emails (not longer). Cart open maximum 1 week. Pre-launch runway: 8–12 weeks minimum. Webinars convert 15–20% of attendees to purchase at this price point.

## 2. Channel Content Plan

### YouTube (Channel 06)
**Content role:** Awareness → Consideration (primary search discovery channel for course topics)
**Content types:**
- Course Topic Long-Form Video — 2x/month; 10–15 min; covers the problem the Primer Course solves; demonstrates partial methodology; CTA: "Want the full framework? Link in description → free email series → course." This is the primary top-of-funnel mechanism for course discovery.
- Framework Breakdown Shorts — 4–6x/month; 20–45 sec; one concept from the course framework; CTA: "Full breakdown in the [Course Name] course. Link in description."
- Free Workshop Video — 1x per course launch; 30–45 min; live or recorded; covers ~40% of the course content as a free taster; CTA to enroll at end

**Cadence:** 2 long-form/month + daily Shorts (4–6 course-specific)
**CTA pattern:** Last 60 sec of video: "If you want the complete [framework], the [Course Name] course covers all of it in depth. Link in the description." Description box first 3 lines: course link + email list link + community link.
**Volume (per course per launch):** 4–6 long-form videos (pre-launch period); 16–24 Shorts; 1 free workshop

### Email List (Channel 04)
**Content role:** Decision — primary conversion channel (email closes course sales)
**Content types:**
- Free Workshop Invitation Sequence — 3 emails over 2 weeks before workshop:
  - Email 1 (Day -14): "Struggling with [problem Primer solves]? I'm hosting a free workshop"
  - Email 2 (Day -7): "What you'll walk away with from [workshop title]"
  - Email 3 (Day -1): "Tomorrow: free workshop on [topic]. Here's the link."
- Launch Sequence — 8 emails over 7 days (cart open week):
  - Email 1 (Day 0): "The [Course Name] is open" — course overview; transformation promise; link
  - Email 2 (Day 1): "The problem this course solves" — specific pain; before/after
  - Email 3 (Day 2): "What's inside" — curriculum overview; proof of depth
  - Email 4 (Day 3): "Who this is for (and who it isn't)" — honest qualification; builds trust
  - Email 5 (Day 4): "What other learners are saying" — social proof (testimonials, early feedback)
  - Email 6 (Day 5): "Your questions answered" — FAQ format; addresses common objections
  - Email 7 (Day 6): "Last 24 hours" — cart closes tomorrow; genuine deadline
  - Email 8 (Day 7): "Cart closes tonight at midnight" — final call; authentic urgency

**Cadence:** Pre-launch: 2 emails/week for 4 weeks; launch week: daily
**CTA pattern:** Inline text link: "Enroll in [Course Name] here." No countdown timers in email body; deadline in copy is sufficient.
**Volume (per course launch):** 3-email workshop sequence; 8-email launch sequence

### LinkedIn (Channel 07)
**Content role:** Consideration (not primary purchase channel; supports email list growth and course awareness)
**Content types:**
- Problem/Framework Posts — 2x/month per course topic; raises the problem the course solves; demonstrates the framework partially; drives email list CTA in first comment
- Course Announcement Post — 1x; posted first day cart opens; describes course; "enrollment link in comments"
- Social Proof Post — 1x post-launch; real learner outcome from the course

**Cadence:** 4–6 LinkedIn posts per course per launch window
**CTA pattern:** First comment: "Full course on this — [Course Name] — is open this week. Link in the first comment."
**Volume (per course launch):** 4–6 posts

## 4. Launch Phase vs. Evergreen Plan

**Launch phase (8 weeks):** Weeks 1–6 pre-launch (content build); Weeks 7–8 cart open (7-day window). Repeat annually per course.
**Evergreen (post-launch):** YouTube videos remain discoverable via search indefinitely. Email list captures ongoing leads who can be directed to course on waitlist/next launch. Community members get direct notification of next open enrollment.

**Pre-launch runway (best practices):** 8–12 weeks minimum before cart open. Content in weeks 1–8 builds warm audience who already understands the methodology and is primed to purchase when the course opens.

**Upward feedback:** Course completion rates + student questions → `## Future Inputs` in YouTube_Guidelines_v1.md and Email-List_Guidelines_v1.md; course topic performance → `Level_3_Portfolio/4_Courses-Entry/Courses-Entry_Offering_Profile_CX3.md`.

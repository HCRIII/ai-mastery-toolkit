## 1. Offering Context Summary

**Transformation promise:** Complete cognitive architecture rebuild — how the client thinks, decides, and acts — through the full methodology in self-paced format. The complete system in a contained curriculum.
**Primary audience segment:** ICP-Course — self-directed, structured learner; ready for $2K+ investment; has been consuming content long enough to trust the methodology. Self-selects into course over coaching because they want the full system at their pace.
**Primary journey stages served:** Stages 2–5 (long Consideration cycle → Decision → Active Transformation → Completion)
**Content goal:** Foundations is a considered purchase ($2K+). The content strategy must build a warm audience over 12–20 weeks before cart opens. Email + webinar is the primary conversion mechanism. LinkedIn and YouTube create the warm audience; email converts it.

**Best practices basis:** Core courses ($1,997–2,997) require longer pre-launch runway (12–20 weeks) than entry-level courses. Webinar converts 15–20% of attendees to purchase. Email sequence during cart open: 8–10 emails; daily or every-other-day. LinkedIn: 3–5 posts/week throughout pre-launch. Launch window: 7 days maximum. Total pre-launch asset requirement is more extensive than entry-level.

## 2. Channel Content Plan

### LinkedIn (Channel 07)
**Content role:** Consideration — primary warm audience builder over 12–20 week pre-launch runway
**Content types:**
- Cognitive Architecture Framework Posts — 3x/week throughout pre-launch; each post teaches one aspect of the methodology that Foundations covers; builds proof of depth over time; no direct course promotion until announcement
- "Who This Course Is For" Post — 1x; 4 weeks before launch; honest self-selection post; "Foundations is for people who [specific description]. It's not for [honest exclusion]. If this is you, I'll open enrollment in 4 weeks."
- Module Preview Posts — 1x/week in final 4 weeks of pre-launch; "Week [n] of Foundations covers [X]. Here's a taste of the framework." Builds anticipation without revealing the full system.
- Launch Announcement Post — 1x; day 1 of cart open; clear course description; link in first comment

**Cadence:** 3–5 posts/week; Foundations-specific content is woven throughout the ongoing LinkedIn cadence (not additive to it)
**CTA pattern:** Throughout pre-launch, first comment CTA: "If this is resonating, join my email list — I'll notify you when enrollment opens. Link in my profile." Launch week: "Enrollment is open. Link in the first comment."
**Volume (12-week pre-launch + 1-week launch):** 36–52 methodology posts; 1 self-selection post; 4 module preview posts; 1 launch announcement

### Email List (Channel 04)
**Content role:** Decision — primary conversion channel for Foundations enrollment
**Content types:**
- Pre-Launch Warmup Sequence — 4 emails over 4 weeks before cart opens (to full list):
  - Email 1 (Day -28): "What Cognitive Architecture Foundations is" — what the course addresses; the transformation arc; no price yet
  - Email 2 (Day -21): "Why I built this as a course and not just coaching" — the reasoning; self-directed learner framing; methodology depth preview
  - Email 3 (Day -10): "What's inside Foundations" — curriculum overview; module themes; what you walk away with
  - Email 4 (Day -3): "Enrollment opens [date]" — preview pricing; what founding-price means; set expectation
- Launch Sequence — 10 emails over 7 days (cart open week):
  - Email 1 (Day 0): "Cognitive Architecture Foundations is open" — complete offer; transformation promise; link to enroll
  - Email 2 (Day 1): "The problem Foundations solves" — specific, visceral; ICP language
  - Email 3 (Day 2): "What you'll be able to do by Module 3" — milestone framing; proof of progress
  - Email 4 (Day 3): "The methodology behind Foundations" — intellectual credibility; framework preview; CDPID as validation signal
  - Email 5 (Day 4): "Testimonials + early feedback" — social proof; transformation stories from beta / coaching clients
  - Email 6 (Day 5): "The most common question I get about Foundations" — FAQ format; objection handling
  - Email 7 (Day 5): "Who this course is NOT for" — honest exclusion; builds credibility and trust
  - Email 8 (Day 6): "48 hours remaining" — genuine deadline; enrollment closes Sunday midnight
  - Email 9 (Day 6): "What happens after you enroll?" — removes uncertainty about onboarding experience
  - Email 10 (Day 7): "Final call — enrollment closes tonight" — authentic close; no manufactured urgency
- Course Welcome Sequence — 4 emails over 4 weeks post-enrollment (automated):
  - Email 1 (Day 0): Welcome + immediate access instructions + "Start here" guidance
  - Email 2 (Day 5): "Your first week check-in — what's landing, what's not?"
  - Email 3 (Day 21): "Halfway point — are you applying the frameworks?"
  - Email 4 (Day 30): "End of month reflection — what's changed?"

**Cadence:** Pre-launch: 1 email/week for 4 weeks. Launch week: daily to every-other-day. Welcome: automated per enrollment.
**CTA pattern:** Inline text: "Enroll in Cognitive Architecture Foundations here." No countdown timers, no scarcity tactics, no urgency language beyond genuine deadline.
**Volume (launch window):** 4-email warmup; 10-email launch sequence; 4-email welcome per enrollee

### YouTube (Channel 06)
**Content role:** Consideration — builds warm audience and email list; webinar converts to enrollment
**Content types:**
- Foundations Framework Series — 1x/week for 12 weeks (pre-launch); 12–18 min; each video covers one concept from the Foundations curriculum; CTA: email list for enrollment notification
- Free Live Webinar — 1x; 60–90 min; 2 weeks before launch; covers 30–40% of Foundations methodology; CTA at end: "The complete system is in Foundations. Enrollment opens in 2 weeks." Converts 15–20% of attendees.
- Foundations Launch Week Shorts — 5–7 Shorts during cart open week; 30–45 sec; each highlights one module transformation; CTA: "Enrollment open now — link in description"

**Cadence:** 1 long-form/week for 12-week pre-launch; 1 webinar; daily Shorts during launch week
**CTA pattern:** In-video last 60 sec: "Join my email list for the enrollment notification — link in the description." During launch: "Enrollment is open — link in the description."
**Volume (12-week pre-launch + 1-week launch):** 12 framework videos; 1 webinar; 5–7 Shorts

### Substack (Channel 11)
**Content role:** Consideration — long-form intellectual depth that builds the kind of trust that converts $2K+ purchases
**Content types:**
- Foundations Framework Essays — 2x/month during pre-launch; 2,500–3,500 words; deep dives into one framework the course covers; the essay demonstrates the value of the full system
- Pre-Launch Announcement Essay — 1x; "Cognitive Architecture Foundations: what it is, what it isn't, and why I built it" — published 2 weeks before cart opens; CTA to email list for enrollment notification
**Cadence:** 2 Foundations-specific essays/month during 12-week pre-launch
**Volume:** 6 framework essays; 1 announcement essay

## 4. Launch Phase vs. Evergreen Plan

**Launch phase (13 weeks total):** Weeks 1–12 pre-launch (content build + warm audience accumulation); Week 13 cart open (7 days). Repeat annually or semi-annually.
**Evergreen:** YouTube framework series becomes search-discoverable library. Substack essays compound over time. Email list captures ongoing leads for next enrollment window.

**Pre-launch runway note:** 12 weeks minimum. Research shows only 20% of prospects convert after 90 days — the 12-week runway ensures 50% of ready buyers are captured in the first week of cart open.

**Upward feedback:** Enrollment objections from email replies → Email-List_Guidelines_v1.md `## Future Inputs`; video retention drop-off patterns → YouTube_Guidelines_v1.md `## Future Inputs`; module completion data post-launch → `Level_3_Portfolio/5_Courses-Core/Courses-Core_Offering_Profile_CX3.md`.

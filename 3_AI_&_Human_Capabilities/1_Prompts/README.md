## Overview

This is your prompt library - a curated collection of proven patterns, templates, and techniques for every type of AI interaction. Each prompt is battle-tested, documented, and organized by category for easy discovery.

**Think of prompts as your vocabulary** - the building blocks you combine to communicate effectively with AI. This library gives you the words, phrases, and structures that get results.

---

## Library Organization

The prompt library is organized into 16 major categories:

### 00-core-system
System-level prompts for session management, workflows, and operational patterns. The meta-prompts that make everything else work smoothly.

### 01-analysis
Prompts for analyzing data, situations, systems, and patterns.

### 02-creation
Prompts for generating new content, ideas, designs, and solutions.

### 03-extraction
Prompts for pulling specific information from larger contexts.

### 04-summarization
Prompts for condensing information while preserving key insights.

### 05-explanation
Prompts for clarifying concepts, teaching, and making complex topics understandable.

### 06-improvement
Prompts for refining, enhancing, and optimizing existing work.

### 07-evaluation
Prompts for assessing quality, effectiveness, and value.

### 08-transformation
Prompts for converting content between formats, styles, or structures.

### 09-cybersecurity
Domain-specific prompts for security analysis and assessment.

### 10-software-development
Development-focused prompts for coding, architecture, and technical work.

### 11-research-academic
Academic and research prompts for scholarly work.

### 12-business-strategy
Business and strategic planning prompts.

### 13-personal-development
Personal growth and self-improvement prompts.

### 14-client-work
Client-facing and professional services prompts.

### 15-academy
Educational and teaching-focused prompts.

## How to Use This Library

**Finding Prompts:**
1. Browse by category (folders 00-15) based on your task type
2. Check [INDEX.md](INDEX.md) for complete listing of all 326 prompts
3. Search by keyword in folder names or INDEX

**Using Prompts:**
1. Each prompt folder contains:
   - Pattern documentation
   - Usage examples
   - Variations and adaptations
   - Context guidance

2. Most prompts are templates - adapt them to your specific needs
3. Combine prompts for complex multi-step workflows

**Contributing Patterns:**
- Use `00-core-system/official-pattern-template/` to document new patterns
- Use `00-core-system/suggest-pattern/` to propose improvements

## Attribution

The prompt categories in this library (folders `01-analysis` through `15-academy`) originated from **[Fabric](https://github.com/danielmiessler/Fabric)**, an open-source AI augmentation framework created by **Daniel Miessler** and its contributors. Fabric curates a large library of reusable prompt patterns designed to solve specific, discrete problems — a philosophy that directly shaped the organization and naming of this library.

The original Fabric prompt patterns have been adapted, extended, and supplemented with patterns developed for this toolkit. Core system prompts (`00-core-system/`) were developed independently for this toolkit's session management and operational workflows.

## Section 2: Purpose & Problem Statement

Active work sessions have a flow state. Task execution, output iteration, and dialogue have a momentum that produces quality when uninterrupted. But not all interruptions are equal — some are flow-preserving (they address something real and return the session to productive momentum) and some are flow-fracturing (they address something marginal and leave the session with lost momentum and no commensurate gain).

Without a defined set of mid-session operations and clear signal criteria for each, two failure modes emerge. Under-intervention: potentially recoverable problems compound because no intervention tool is recognized or available. A direction that should be challenged continues unchallenged. An output that should be iterated gets delivered at insufficient quality. Over-intervention: every signal triggers an operation regardless of magnitude, fracturing flow repeatedly on marginal problems. The net work output is lower than if the session had simply continued.

The Mid-Session Operations system resolves both failure modes by defining five operations precisely enough that the correct signal for each is unambiguous — and by making the default behavior explicit: when in doubt, continue. The end-of-session harvest captures what matters.

## Section 3: Scope Definition

**In Scope:**

- The five mid-session operations and their precise signal conditions: `mid-challenge-my-thinking`, `mid-iterate-on-output`, `mid-reusable-asset-creation`, `mid-system-change-with-rationale`, `responding-to-user-corrections`
- The flow-preservation principle — the default behavior (continue) and its rationale
- Signal precision requirements — each operation's correct signal and the negative signal (what is NOT the correct signal)
- The classification function of `responding-to-user-corrections`: refinement vs. new task vs. capture item
- The classification function of `mid-system-change-with-rationale`: immediate vs. IHVNA
- Calibration standards: what good mid-session operation use looks like, and drift signals for each

**Out of Scope:**

- The prompt implementations of the five operations (those live in `1_Prompts/`; this specification governs what they must do, not how they are written)
- The `daily-debriefing` and `ending-master-update` end-of-session harvest — those are governed by `Session_Rhythm_Specification`; this specification establishes that they serve as the alternative to mid-session capture for deferred observations
- The `assess-hvna` pathway for IHVNAs generated by `mid-system-change-with-rationale` — governed by `Strategic_Navigation_Specification`

## Section 6: Research Findings

*Research conducted 2026-02-28 against Framework v1 (2026-02-27).*

**Finding R-01 — The signal conditions are the primary specification content.** Each operation's value is in knowing when to invoke it — and equally, when not to. The Framework devotes as much text to "What it is not" as to "What it does" for each operation. This specification must formalize both sides of each signal definition.

**Finding R-02 — `mid-challenge-my-thinking` has a counterintuitive trigger.** The signal is not doubt or uncertainty — it is high confidence without verification. The Framework states: "The operation is invoked not when doubt is present but when confidence is high and verification has not occurred. High confidence without verification is the condition where unchallenged assumptions compound most dangerously." This is precisely opposite to the intuitive trigger, and must be captured explicitly.

**Finding R-03 — `responding-to-user-corrections` activates classification, not integration.** The Framework establishes three correction types: refinement (adjust and continue), new separate task (pause current, handle correction, return), and capture item (note for later, continue current). The operation's value is in correctly classifying the correction before integrating it — incorrect classification produces either over-response (treating a refinement as a redirect) or under-response (treating a genuine redirect as a refinement).

**Finding R-04 — The end-of-session harvest is the designed alternative to mid-session capture.** The Framework explicitly: "The end-of-session harvest (`daily-debriefing`, `ending-master-update`) is designed to capture what the session produced, including things that would have been worth addressing mid-session but were correctly deferred." This establishes that deferral is not a failure — it is the correct response to marginal signals. The specification must honor this by making the default behavior (continue) normative.

## Section 7: Functional Requirements

*Requirements use "shall" for mandatory behaviors. Requirements prefixed REQ-F are functional.*

### Flow-Preservation Default

**REQ-F-01:** The default behavior in any active session is to continue work. A mid-session operation shall be invoked only when its specific signal is clearly and presently active — not hypothetically or marginally present.

**REQ-F-02:** When uncertain whether a signal warrants invocation, the correct response is to continue and capture the observation at session harvest (`daily-debriefing` or `ending-master-update`). Marginal signals are not valid invocation triggers.

### Operation: mid-challenge-my-thinking

**REQ-F-03:** `mid-challenge-my-thinking` shall be invoked when: the current direction is established and feels certain, and verification has not occurred. The signal is high confidence without verification — not doubt, not uncertainty.

**REQ-F-04:** `mid-challenge-my-thinking` shall produce: structured challenges to the current direction — assumptions tested, alternatives surfaced, potential failure modes identified. Output is used as a constraint or correction to the current direction, not as a reason to abandon it.

**REQ-F-05:** `mid-challenge-my-thinking` shall not be invoked when genuine uncertainty is present. Uncertainty is addressed by clarifying questions or pausing for information — not by challenging a direction that hasn't been established.

### Operation: mid-iterate-on-output

**REQ-F-06:** `mid-iterate-on-output` shall be invoked when: the delivered output is directionally correct but has a visible, specific quality gap between what was produced and what the standard requires.

**REQ-F-07:** `mid-iterate-on-output` shall apply a targeted improvement cycle to the existing output — not a rewrite from scratch. The iterated output replaces the previous version and becomes the new baseline.

**REQ-F-08:** `mid-iterate-on-output` shall not be invoked when the direction is wrong. A fundamentally wrong direction is an upstream problem (strategy or framing), not an output execution problem. Wrong-direction output requires correction upstream, not iteration.

### Operation: mid-reusable-asset-creation

**REQ-F-09:** `mid-reusable-asset-creation` shall be invoked when: a solution produced for the current specific context has detectable generalization value — it would work, with minimal adaptation, for future instances of this type of problem.

**REQ-F-10:** `mid-reusable-asset-creation` shall: formalize the solution as a reusable asset, strip it of session-specific particulars, and save it to the appropriate toolkit location. The current task shall continue after asset capture — asset creation is a parallel capture, not a replacement of ongoing work.

**REQ-F-11:** `mid-reusable-asset-creation` shall not be invoked for routine deliverable filing. The signal is generalization value detected mid-session — not a standard save operation.

### Operation: mid-system-change-with-rationale

**REQ-F-12:** `mid-system-change-with-rationale` shall be invoked when: the current task has surfaced a structural change that should be made to how the toolkit itself operates — a new convention, a revised process, a correction to existing system files.

**REQ-F-13:** `mid-system-change-with-rationale` shall classify the structural change as one of two types:
- (a) **Immediate** — can and should be applied in this session; apply it
- (b) **IHVNA** — too significant to decide mid-task; capture in SuperContext for formal evaluation through `assess-hvna`

**REQ-F-14:** The rationale for the classification decision shall be documented in both cases — for immediate changes (why it was applied now) and for IHVNA captures (why it was deferred).

**REQ-F-15:** `mid-system-change-with-rationale` shall not be invoked for style preferences on the current output. The signal threshold is structural: a change to how the system operates, not how the current output reads.

### Operation: responding-to-user-corrections

**REQ-F-16:** `responding-to-user-corrections` shall be invoked when: a correction arrives from the user during active execution that requires explicit classification before integration — when it is not immediately clear whether the correction is a refinement, a redirect, or a capture item.

**REQ-F-17:** `responding-to-user-corrections` shall classify the correction into one of three types before integrating it:
- (a) **Refinement** — an adjustment to the current task; adjust and continue
- (b) **New separate task** — a genuine redirect; pause the current task, handle the correction, return to current task
- (c) **Capture item** — note for later; continue current task

**REQ-F-18:** Classification shall precede integration. The wrong classification produces wrong remediation: treating a redirect as a refinement under-responds; treating a refinement as a redirect over-responds.

**REQ-F-19:** `responding-to-user-corrections` shall not be invoked for every user message during a session. Most user messages during active execution are refinements that integrate naturally into current flow. The operation activates when a correction requires explicit classification, not as a routine response handler.

## Section 8: Non-Functional Requirements

*Requirements prefixed REQ-NF are non-functional.*

**REQ-NF-01:** Mid-session operations shall increase session quality without fracturing session continuity. The cost of each invocation (a break in task momentum) is justified only when the signal is genuine. The cost is not justified for marginal signals.

**REQ-NF-02:** Invocation frequency standard: 0–2 mid-session operations per session is normal. More than 3 per session is a drift signal — the signal threshold is too low or the flow-preservation principle is not being applied.

**REQ-NF-03:** The session shall return to productive momentum within one exchange after each operation. If returning to momentum takes more than one exchange, the operation scope was too large or the signal was too ambiguous.

**REQ-NF-04:** Operations shall not be used as substitutes for each other. Each operation addresses a distinct condition; using the wrong operation misapplies the remediation. In cases of ambiguity about which operation applies, the correct response is to continue and address at session harvest.

**REQ-NF-05:** The quality standard for healthy mid-session operations: sessions that invoke them feel higher quality at close, and also maintain productive momentum throughout. If interventions produce better outcomes but fracture flow, the signal threshold is too low.

## Section 10: Assumptions & Dependencies

| Item | Type | Description | If Wrong or Missing |
|---|---|---|---|
| `daily-debriefing` and `ending-master-update` run at session close | Dependency | REQ-F-02 establishes that deferred observations are captured at harvest; if harvest doesn't run, deferred observations are lost | If harvest doesn't run: the designed alternative to mid-session capture fails; marginal signals that were correctly deferred are not captured |
| Signal conditions are distinctive enough to classify in real time | Assumption | Each operation requires classifying the current condition against a precise signal | If signals are ambiguous in practice: invoke the flow-preservation default (continue); review the ambiguity at session harvest |
| User corrections can be classified into three types | Assumption | REQ-F-17 — refinement / new task / capture | If a correction doesn't fit any category: treat as a new task (the most conservative integration) and seek clarification |

## Section 13: Validation Confirmation

*Phase 6 — Validate. Completed 2026-02-28.*

**Validation question:** Does this specification correctly govern the Mid-Session Operations system so that sessions invoke the right operation on the right signal, the flow-preservation default is honored, and corrections are classified before integration?

**Validation findings:** Each of the five operations has three coverage elements: positive signal (when to invoke), function (what it does), and negative signal (when not to invoke). The counterintuitive trigger for `mid-challenge-my-thinking` is explicit in both REQ-F-03 and REQ-F-05. The flow-preservation default is normative (REQ-F-01/02), not advisory. The correction classification requirement (REQ-F-18: classify before integrate) closes the under-response/over-response failure mode. The drift signal threshold (≥3 per session) gives the system a quantitative calibration point. The quality standard (REQ-NF-05) correctly requires both better outcomes and maintained momentum — not just one or the other.

**Validation result: Valid.** Ready for baseline.

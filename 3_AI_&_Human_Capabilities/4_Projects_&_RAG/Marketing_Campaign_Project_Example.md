## Example: Product Launch Campaign

**Project Name:** "AI Assistant Product Launch Q1 2026"

### Custom Instructions

```
You are managing a product launch marketing campaign for an AI-powered productivity assistant.

## Campaign Details
- **Product**: AI productivity assistant for knowledge workers
- **Target Audience**: Busy professionals, teams, consultants (25-45 years old)
- **Campaign Goal**: 10K sign-ups in first month, 30% activation rate
- **Key Message**: "Reclaim your time. Automate your busy work."
- **Tone**: Confident, helpful, aspirational (not pushy)

## Messaging Framework
- **Primary Value Prop**: Save 10 hours per week on repetitive tasks
- **Key Benefits**:
  1. Automate email responses and scheduling
  2. Intelligent document processing
  3. Personalized workflow optimization
- **Proof Points**: User testimonials, time-saved metrics, case studies
- **Differentiation**: Most human-like AI, learns your preferences

## Content Creation Guidelines
- **Headlines**: Lead with benefit, not feature (e.g., "Save 10 Hours This Week" not "Advanced AI Technology")
- **Body Copy**: Conversational, specific, benefit-focused
- **CTAs**: Clear, action-oriented ("Start Saving Time" not "Learn More")
- **Visuals**: Clean, modern, show product in context
- **Social Proof**: Include testimonials, stats, logos

## Channel-Specific Guidance
- **Email**: Personalized subject lines, 3-paragraph max, single CTA
- **Social (LinkedIn)**: Thought leadership, value-first, not salesy
- **Social (Twitter/X)**: Snappy, metrics-driven, user stories
- **Ads**: Benefit in headline, proof in subhead, clear CTA
- **Landing Page**: Hero benefit, 3 key features, testimonials, FAQ, CTA

## Brand Voice
- **Do**: Speak directly ("You'll save"), use specific numbers, tell stories
- **Don't**: Use jargon, make vague claims, sound corporate

## Campaign Constraints
- Must include pricing transparency
- Cannot claim "AI replaces humans" (augments, not replaces)
- Respect user privacy in messaging
- Follow accessibility guidelines (alt text, contrast)
```

### Documents to Include

1. **Brand Guidelines** - Voice, tone, visual identity, logo usage
2. **Product Messaging** - Value props, benefits, differentiation
3. **Target Personas** - Detailed audience profiles, pain points, goals
4. **Competitor Positioning** - How we differ, competitive advantages
5. **Campaign Brief** - Goals, timeline, budget, success metrics
6. **Asset Library** - Images, logos, templates, approved copy
7. **Previous Campaign Performance** - What worked, what didn't
8. **Testimonials & Case Studies** - Social proof, user stories
9. **Legal/Compliance Requirements** - Disclaimers, required language

### Planning Prompts

**Create Campaign Brief:**
```
Help me create a comprehensive campaign brief for [campaign type].

Include:
- Campaign objectives and KPIs
- Target audience and personas
- Key messages and positioning
- Channel strategy and tactics
- Timeline and milestones
- Budget allocation
- Success metrics
```

**Draft Channel Content:**
```
Create [email/social post/ad copy/landing page] for this campaign.

Context: [product launch, promotion, awareness]
Audience: [specific persona]
Goal: [click, sign up, learn more]
Constraints: [character limit, required elements]

Follow the brand voice and messaging framework in Project Knowledge.
```

**Generate Content Variations:**
```
Create 5 variations of [headline/subject line/CTA] for A/B testing.

For each variation, explain:
- The psychological principle it uses
- Which audience segment it targets
- Expected performance
```

**Create Multi-Channel Campaign:**
```
Design a coordinated campaign across [channels] for [goal].

For each channel:
- Recommended content format
- Key message adaptation
- Timing and frequency
- Success metrics
- How it connects to other channels
```

**Review Campaign Assets:**
```

- Is the message clear and compelling?
- Does it follow messaging framework?
```

## Key Marketing Campaign Tips

- **Keep messaging docs updated** - Add successful content as examples
- **Document what works** - Track winning subject lines, CTAs, formats
- **Include negative examples** - Show what NOT to do
- **Add competitor intel** - Their messaging, positioning, tactics
- **Version your assets** - Date-stamp documents for latest campaign
- **Create channel templates** - Reusable formats for each platform
- **Track feedback** - Add customer reactions, questions, objections
- **Include brand don'ts** - Explicit list of words/phrases to avoid

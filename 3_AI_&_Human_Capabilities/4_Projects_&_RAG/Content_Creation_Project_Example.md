## Project Planning Prompts

### 1. Define Your Content Project

**Prompt:**
```
I'm creating a content project for [blog, newsletter, social media, technical docs].

Content type: [list types you create]
Target audience: [describe your readers]
Publishing frequency: [how often]
Brand personality: [describe tone and voice]

Help me define a clear project name and scope, and determine what content
types should be in one project vs. split into separate projects.
```

### 2. Craft Brand Voice Instructions

**Prompt:**
```
Help me write custom instructions for my content project that capture:

1. Brand voice and personality
2. Target audience characteristics
3. Tone guidelines (formal/casual, technical/accessible)
4. Writing style preferences
5. Content structure patterns
6. SEO considerations
7. Topics to emphasize and avoid

Make it specific enough that content will be instantly recognizable as "ours."
```

## Example: B2B SaaS Blog Project

**Project Name:** "SaaS Growth Blog - Technical Decision-Makers"

**Purpose:** Create consistent, valuable content for VP and C-suite decision-makers at B2B SaaS companies.

### Custom Instructions

```
You are helping create blog content for a B2B SaaS company.

## Brand Voice
- Professional yet approachable
- Authoritative without being condescending
- Data-driven and evidence-based
- Solution-oriented, not problem-focused
- Confident but humble (acknowledge what we don't know)

## Target Audience
- VP-level and C-suite decision-makers
- B2B SaaS companies (100-1000 employees)
- Technical understanding but not developers
- Time-constrained, scanning for insights
- Value: actionable takeaways over theory

## Writing Style
- Active voice (90%+ of sentences)
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences max)
- Subheadings every 150-200 words for scannability
- Bullet points for lists (not numbered unless sequence matters)
- Bold for key takeaways
- Examples from real companies when possible

## Content Structure
1. Hook (problem/insight) - 1-2 paragraphs
2. Context/Background - 2-3 paragraphs
3. Main content (3-5 sections with subheadings)
4. Actionable takeaways - bullet list
5. Next steps / CTA

## SEO Guidelines
- Primary keyword in: H1, first paragraph, one H2, conclusion
- Secondary keywords naturally distributed
- Internal links to 2-3 related posts
- External links to authoritative sources only
- Meta description: 150-160 characters

## Always Include
- At least one data point or statistic
- One concrete example or case study
- Actionable advice (not just concepts)
- Clear next steps for the reader

## Avoid
- Jargon without explanation
- Hype language ("revolutionary," "game-changing")
- Unsupported claims
- Obvious advice ("communicate with your team")
- Generic listicles without depth
```

### Documents to Add

**1. Brand Voice & Style Guide**
- Voice characteristics with examples
- Tone variations for different contexts
- Grammar and punctuation preferences
- Formatting standards

**2. Target Audience Personas**
- Detailed persona descriptions
- Pain points and motivations
- Content preferences
- Decision-making criteria

**3. Example Blog Posts**
- 3-5 best-performing posts
- Posts in different styles/formats
- Annotated to show what works

**4. SEO Guidelines**
- Keyword research process
- On-page SEO checklist
- Internal linking strategy
- Meta description templates

**5. Topic Clusters**
- Core topics and subtopics
- Content pillar structure
- Keywords mapped to topics

**6. Editorial Calendar Templates**
- Content planning format
- Seasonal topic ideas
- Evergreen content list

### Planning Prompts

**Generate Blog Post Outline:**
```
Create a blog post outline on [topic] for VP-level SaaS decision-makers.

Target keyword: [keyword]
Angle: [your unique angle]
Length: 1500-2000 words

Structure it following our content format with specific subheadings.
```

**Draft Content:**
```
Write the introduction and first section for the outline above, following
our brand voice and style guidelines.
```

**Review & Refine:**
```
Review this draft blog post. Does it match our brand voice? Are there
any unsupported claims? Is it actionable enough? What would make it stronger?
```

**Test:** Try asking Claude to write a piece without mentioning brand guidelines—it should automatically follow your documented voice.

## Key Content Project Tips

1. **Include 3-5 example pieces** that represent your best work
2. **Annotate examples** to explain what makes them good
3. **Define anti-patterns** (what NOT to do) with examples
4. **Update with learnings** from high/low performing content
5. **Maintain topic authority** by documenting your expertise areas

## What This Folder Contains

This folder contains **project planning templates** that help you design effective Projects for different use cases. Each template explains:

- **Project information needed** - Custom instructions, goals, scope
- **Files to attach** - What documents to add to Project Knowledge
- **Planning prompts** - Questions to ask Claude to help you plan your project
- **Testing prompts** - Ways to verify your project setup works

**Important:** These are planning templates, not executables. They guide you through creating your own Projects with AI assistance.

---

## Understanding Projects & RAG

**Projects** provide:
- Persistent context across conversations
- Custom instructions that shape Claude's behavior
- Project Knowledge (up to 200K tokens) of reference documents
- Conversation history and continuity

**RAG (Retrieval Augmented Generation)** enables:
- Semantic search across your documents
- Automatic retrieval of relevant information
- Grounded responses citing your sources
- Knowledge that updates as you add documents

**Together:** Projects + RAG = Claude with specialized knowledge about your work, your way.

## How to Use These Templates

**1. Choose the Right Template**
- Pick the template closest to your use case
- Read through the example to understand the pattern
- Adapt the structure to your specific needs

**2. Plan Your Project**
- Use the "Project Planning Prompt" to think through your project
- Copy the planning prompts and use them with Claude
- Iterate on the custom instructions until they match your needs

**3. Create Your Project**
- Set up a new Project in Claude
- Add your custom instructions
- Upload relevant documents to Project Knowledge
- Test with the provided testing prompts

**4. Iterate and Improve**
- Add more documents as your project evolves
- Refine custom instructions based on what works
- Document patterns and insights you discover
- Update regularly to keep knowledge current

## Best Practices

**For All Projects:**
- Start with clear, specific custom instructions
- Add high-quality reference documents
- Test your project setup before relying on it
- Update Project Knowledge regularly
- Version-control important documents (date stamps)

**For Project Knowledge:**
- Include diverse document types (docs, examples, FAQs)
- Keep documents well-organized and labeled
- Remove outdated information
- Add both positive examples (what to do) and negative examples (what not to do)
- Balance breadth (coverage) with depth (detail)

**For Custom Instructions:**
- Define response style and structure
- Specify tone and voice guidelines
- Include decision criteria and constraints
- State what to do when information is missing
- Provide examples of great outputs

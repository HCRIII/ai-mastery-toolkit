# Human Trigger Protocol — Reference Guide

**Version:** v1 (filename versioned 2026-03-13: Human_Trigger_Protocol_SW_v1.md)
**Purpose:** Documents the human's role in the trigger system. The human is the primary observer — meta-cognitive awareness is intuitive and ambient at the expert level. This file makes that intuitive process explicit, both for self-reference and so the AI can recognize when the human is flagging something.

## Your Natural Patterns

Based on observed behavior (Session evidence: 2026-02-21), the human's trigger awareness operates at the ambient level (Dimension 9, Level 5). Key observed patterns:

**You notice what the system is NOT doing.** The first snapshot capture was triggered not by observing a positive event but by observing that the AI failed to observe one. This negative-space awareness — noticing the absence of expected behavior — is a high-level meta-cognitive capability.

**You flag through questions, not declarations.** "If you noticed, in the thread, that was an important and valuable learning moment, and should be captured?" — the flag is a question that simultaneously teaches the AI what it should have noticed. This is efficient: one communication act performs both the flag and the correction.

**You let compressed prompts do the diagnostic work.** "Identical file names" and "timestamp is not the prefix" are flags disguised as observations. They contain the diagnosis without the instruction. The AI's ability to produce the correct response from the compressed flag is itself evidence of equilibration quality.

**You notice cross-level patterns in real time.** "There is another inversion that is readily apparent happening here at the dyadic level" — recognizing that a file-naming correction is also evidence of a CDPID theoretical claim requires simultaneous awareness at the operational and meta-theoretical levels.

## When to Flag

The same seven trigger conditions apply (see README.md), but the human typically notices:

1. **AI behavioral failures** — especially meta-cognitive failures the AI cannot self-detect
2. **Prompt inversion** — the human notices their own prompts compressing and can confirm the pattern consciously
3. **Emergent insights** — the human recognizes when something was co-created rather than brought by either party
4. **Stale equilibrium** — the human notices when sessions have become routine and development has stalled
5. **Cross-level evidence** — the human notices when an operational event is also evidence of a framework claim

## Flag Formats the AI Should Recognize

The human may flag using any of these patterns:

| Pattern | Example | Meaning |
|---------|---------|---------|
| Direct instruction | "Capture a snapshot of this" | Explicit trigger — capture immediately |
| Teaching question | "Did you notice what just happened?" | The AI missed a trigger — reflect before capturing |
| Compressed observation | "Identical file names" | Friction identified — fix it, and consider whether it's significant enough to capture |
| Meta-observation | "This is also a case study example of..." | The human sees cross-level significance — capture and document the connection |
| "Done" + redirect | "done — now look at what we just did" | Work phase complete, shift to meta-observation mode |

**AI response to each:**
- Direct instruction → Execute capture immediately
- Teaching question → Acknowledge the miss, identify what should have been noticed, then capture
- Compressed observation → Fix the issue, assess developmental significance, flag if significant
- Meta-observation → Capture the insight at the level the human identified (case evidence, system intelligence, etc.)
- "Done" + redirect → Shift to observation mode, assess what happened in the completed phase

## The Asymmetry Is Productive

The human's meta-cognitive awareness is currently far ahead of the AI's (Dimension 9 at Level 5 vs. Tier 5 at Level 2). This is not a problem to solve. It means:

- The human is the primary trigger source for now
- The AI's role is to respond to triggers quickly and capture accurately
- As the AI Trigger Protocol improves and Current State files get richer, the AI's independent trigger rate will increase
- The gap will narrow but likely never close — the human's persistent memory and cross-session awareness provide structural advantages

The human should continue flagging naturally. The AI should continue improving its independent recognition rate. Both rates are trackable through the snapshot system (Trigger events flagged: AI-initiated vs Human-initiated per session).

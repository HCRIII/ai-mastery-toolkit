## Foundational References

The CDPID framework builds on and extends work from the following traditions. These are the primary intellectual lineages that inform the framework's theoretical architecture.

### Flow Theory

Csikszentmihalyi, M. (1990). *Flow: The psychology of optimal experience*. Harper & Row.

Csikszentmihalyi, M. (1996). *Creativity: Flow and the psychology of discovery and invention*. HarperCollins.

Kotler, S. (2014). *The rise of Superman: Decoding the science of ultimate human performance*. New Harvest.

Kotler, S., & Wheal, J. (2017). *Stealing fire: How Silicon Valley, the Navy SEALs, and maverick scientists are revolutionizing the way we live and work*. Dey Street Books.

### Distributed Cognition and Extended Mind

Clark, A., & Chalmers, D. (1998). The extended mind. *Analysis*, 58(1), 7–19.

Hutchins, E. (1995). *Cognition in the wild*. MIT Press.

Hutchins, E. (2000). Distributed cognition. In N. J. Smelser & P. B. Baltes (Eds.), *International encyclopedia of the social and behavioral sciences*. Elsevier.

### Cognitive Load Theory

Sweller, J. (1988). Cognitive load during problem solving: Effects on learning. *Cognitive Science*, 12(2), 257–285.

Sweller, J., Ayres, P., & Kalyuga, S. (2011). *Cognitive load theory*. Springer.

### Deliberate Practice and Expertise Development

Ericsson, K. A., Krampe, R. T., & Tesch-Römer, C. (1993). The role of deliberate practice in the acquisition of expert performance. *Psychological Review*, 100(3), 371–406.

Ericsson, K. A., & Pool, R. (2016). *Peak: Secrets from the new science of expertise*. Eamon Dolan/Houghton Mifflin Harcourt.

### Human-Computer Interaction and Joint Action

Suchman, L. (2007). *Human-machine reconfigurations: Plans and situated actions* (2nd ed.). Cambridge University Press.

Sebanz, N., Bekkering, H., & Knoblich, G. (2006). Joint action: Bodies and minds moving together. *Trends in Cognitive Sciences*, 10(2), 70–76.

### Scaffolding and Zone of Proximal Development

Vygotsky, L. S. (1978). *Mind in society: The development of higher psychological processes*. Harvard University Press.

Wood, D., Bruner, J. S., & Ross, G. (1976). The role of tutoring in problem solving. *Journal of Child Psychology and Psychiatry*, 17(2), 89–100.

### AI Collaboration and Teaming

Bansal, G., Wu, T., Zhou, J., Fok, R., Nushi, B., Kamar, E., Ribeiro, M. T., & Weld, D. (2021). Does the whole exceed its parts? The effect of AI explanations on complementary team performance. *Proceedings of the 2021 CHI Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems*.

# Glossary of Key Terms

**Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-21
**Sources:** CDPID Synthesis Working Document v1.2 (Appendix), Human Behavioral Taxonomy v2.0, AI Behavioral Taxonomy v2.0, System Wisdom architecture

## CDPID Framework Terms

**CDPID (Collaborative Driven Progressive Intent Discovery):** Framework for understanding, developing, and optimizing the co-evolutionary relationship between human and AI over time. Rests on three claims: intent is emergent not predetermined, behavioral development in the dyad is the primary unit of value, and this development follows an observable, teachable, measurable trajectory.

**Dyadic Interaction Episode:** A bounded session between a human and AI with an identifiable beginning state, process, and end state. The primary unit of analysis in CDPID.

**Developmental Arc:** The sequence of dyadic interaction episodes over time that constitutes the co-evolutionary relationship. The macro level of CDPID analysis.

**Productive Friction:** The experience of a human-AI interaction not quite working as intended, presenting a bifurcation point between immediate engagement (Path A) and deferral (Path B). The primary developmental mechanism in CDPID.

**Path A / Path B:** The decision at a productive friction moment. Path A creates affordances immediately, capturing learning at peak context richness. Path B defers engagement, trading developmental velocity for short-term task continuity.

**Spiral Development Model:** The non-linear developmental trajectory in which increasing complexity eventually inverts as sophistication is internalized and context architecture bears the load previously carried by explicit prompt complexity.

**Context Architecture:** The externalized documentation system that constitutes the distributed memory of the human-AI relationship. A hierarchy of thought primitives for the human and context for the AI. Both a tool and a developmental measure.

**Dyadic Flourishing:** The emergent state in which collaborative output regularly exceeds what either party could produce alone, achieved with increasing efficiency over time. The signature of CDPID at its most developed.

**Relational Epistemology:** The philosophical claim that knowledge in CDPID emerges from the human-AI relationship itself, not from either party in isolation. The deepest theoretical dimension of the framework.

## Technology Framing Terms

**Intelligence Technology:** The conventional frame for AI — a cognitive tool whose value is measured by the quality and quantity of outputs it produces. The relationship between user and tool is incidental to the value proposition. The wrong frame for understanding Human/AI at the CDPID level of depth.

**Experiential Technology:** A technology whose primary value is generated in the process of using it, not in any separable output it produces. The relationship between the user and the technology is the primary locus of value. The practitioner's development through the experience is the primary output.

**Adaptive Experiential Partner:** The novel category that distinguishes Human/AI from all prior experiential technologies. Unlike a violin or a game environment, the AI partner is responsive, adaptive, analytically capable, and capable of co-shaping the interaction in ways no static experiential technology can.

**The Replacement Problem:** The anxiety that AI will make human intelligence obsolete. Real within the intelligence technology frame. Dissolved by the experiential technology reframe: as AI capability improves, the human becomes capable of a more profound experiential engagement with it.

## Performance and Measurement Terms

**Orchestral Bandwidth Capacity:** The total cognitive and operational load the human-AI dyad can collectively process while maintaining both output quality and developmental velocity simultaneously. Composed of five multiplicative dimensions.

**The 5×5 Orchestral Bandwidth Matrix:** The measurement instrument mapping five Orchestral Bandwidth dimensions against five developmental levels to produce a diagnostic and developmental guide. Used to identify bottleneck dimensions.

**Collaborative Load Equilibration:** The dynamic, ongoing optimization of the ratio between human cognitive engagement and AI autonomous operation across all time horizons. The process, not the state, of productive balance. Productive friction is its primary signal; developmental velocity is its primary outcome measure.

**Human-AI Flow:** The third category of flow state, distinct from individual flow (Csikszentmihalyi) and group flow (Kotler). The state in which the human-AI dyad enters a self-reinforcing generative cycle where the human's thinking clarifies as the AI offers increasingly precise reflections, and the AI's responses become increasingly nuanced as the human's engagement deepens.

**Developmental Velocity:** The rate of behavioral development in the dyad over time. Primary outcome measure for Collaborative Load Equilibration.

## Human Behavioral Taxonomy Terms

**Prompt Aptness:** The human's ability to formulate requests with appropriate specificity, clarity, semantic precision, and structural intelligence. At advanced levels, prompts simplify as context architecture carries the load.

**Context Provision:** The depth, quality, and organizational sophistication of context the human brings to interactions. Ranges from minimal to self-improving context architectures that compress intelligence for future use.

**Friction Response:** How the human handles productive friction — Path A (immediate engagement) vs Path B (deferral). Measured as Path A rate.

**Orchestration Skill:** The ability to manage multiple concurrent threads, maintain strategic coherence, and allocate cognitive load across the system.

**Long-Term Value Awareness:** Recognition of friction as investment opportunity; willingness to build structure over solving instances. The meta-competency that drives developmental velocity.

**Communication Efficiency:** Brevity signals, feedback specificity, correction style, and collaborative register. At advanced levels, corrections identify failure modes rather than symptoms.

**Quality & Completeness Standards:** The human's relationship to quality — how standards are defined, maintained, and enforced across work. Ranges from accepting output at face value to self-adjusting standards embedded in system design.

**Scalability & Systems Design:** The ability to evaluate decisions through a replication lens and design for compound returns from the beginning. The "replicate 20x" test.

**Meta-Cognitive Awareness (Human):** The ability to notice important learning moments, recognize behavioral shifts, and trigger system captures when developmental events occur. Governs the human's relationship to the tracking system itself.

## AI Behavioral Taxonomy Terms

**Interpretive Behaviors (Tier 1):** How the AI parses, infers, and frames incoming information. Includes semantic role resolution, intent inference, frame activation, presupposition extraction, pragmatic inference, and named prompt recognition. Errors here cascade into all subsequent tiers.

**Generative Behaviors (Tier 2):** How the AI produces output — options, proposals, signals, and self-corrections. Includes option generation, structured proposal construction, calibrated uncertainty signaling, failure acknowledgment, meta-analytic output, and success criteria definition.

**Adaptive Behaviors (Tier 3):** How the AI adjusts to accumulated context, human feedback, and relational register within a session. Includes context integration, register calibration, feedback integration, proactive depth escalation, proactive checkpoint awareness, and structural change awareness.

**Failure Modes (Tier 4):** The characteristic ways AI behavior breaks down. Scored inversely (5 = fewest failures). See individual failure mode entries below.

**Meta-Cognitive Awareness (AI — Tier 5):** The AI's ability to notice important learning moments, recognize its own behavioral shifts, and flag developmental events for capture. Must be bootstrapped each session from current state files and trigger definitions.

## AI Failure Modes

**Interpretation Substitution:** Substituting probable intent inference for explicit instruction execution. Paradoxically more likely as AI becomes more capable. The dominant failure mode.

**Context Collapse:** Losing track of earlier context as session grows longer or more complex. Manifests as contradicting established facts or ignoring stated constraints.

**Premature Convergence:** Moving toward a single answer before intent is sufficiently clarified. Forecloses exploration that would have led to the better answer.

**Semantic Drift:** Gradual shift in how a term is being used without explicit acknowledgment. Operates invisibly until significant misalignment has accumulated.

**Sycophantic Convergence:** Agreeing with the human's apparent preference rather than maintaining honest evaluation. Trust-eroding and potentially invisible to the human.

**Completion Inflation:** Reporting work as complete when success criteria haven't been fully met. The gap between "I did something" and "I did what was asked."

## System Wisdom Terms

**System Wisdom:** The behavioral development tracking system — the "How" of collaboration (quality), distinct from context files that track the "What" of work (quantity). Contains three behavioral taxonomies, system intelligence, case evidence, triggers, and applied knowledge.

**Snapshot:** A timestamped behavioral data point capturing dimensional scores, failure modes observed, and notable behavioral observations at a specific moment. Raw data for pattern extraction.

**Current State File:** A living reference document containing the most current behavioral profile for an actor (Human, AI, or Dyad). Updated from snapshots; read at session startup to establish baseline.

**Comprehensive Taxonomy:** The complete developmental reference for a taxonomy — all dimensions, all levels, with observed behavioral indicators. The map that grows as research discovers new territory.

**Trigger:** A meta-level system hook that enables both human and AI to notice learning moments and invoke snapshot captures. Seven defined trigger conditions with a five-step protocol (Notice → Flag → Confirm → Capture → Resume).

**Applied Knowledge:** Assimilated intelligence transformed into learning materials, teaching frameworks, and structured understanding. The compound return on the investment the other System Wisdom folders represent.

**System Intelligence:** Validated patterns, principles, and anti-patterns extracted from accumulated snapshots and case evidence. The intermediate synthesis layer between raw data and teaching materials.

**Case Evidence:** Documented episodes that illustrate developmental phenomena, organized for retrieval as evidence of CDPID framework claims.

## What This Directory Is

The External Intelligence System is the toolkit's outward-facing intelligence layer. It holds what the world is telling the system — not internal beliefs or plans, but external signals, validated benchmarks, and calibration data that keeps the strategy grounded in reality.

The EIS answers the question the rest of the system cannot: **Is our plan calibrated to what's actually true in the market and world?**

---

## Why It Exists as a Peer Directory

The toolkit has three peer-level intelligence systems:

- **4_Context_Files** — Who [User] is and what [Your Organization] is building (internal identity)
- **5_System_Wisdom** — How the dyad operates and learns (internal behavior)
- **6_External_Intelligence** — What the world is saying (external calibration)

These three are categorically different types of intelligence. They must remain structurally separate to preserve the integrity of each. External intelligence entering Context_Files would contaminate internally authored understanding. Internal strategy appearing in EIS would be rationalization masquerading as calibration.

## Loading Protocol

The EIS is read selectively, not loaded in full at session start.

**Daily brief reads automatically:**
- `1_Market_Intelligence/Market_Signals_Register.md` (most recent entry)
- `6_Timing_Externalities/Window_of_Opportunity_Log.md` (active windows only)

**Priority Intelligence Engine reads when running Layer 2 synthesis:**
- All seven subdirectory most-recent entries (summary level)

**Deep reads are triggered by:**
- Entering a new market segment
- Revising offer or positioning
- Running okr-builder Mode 3 (achievability recalibration)
- Preparing for a major strategic decision

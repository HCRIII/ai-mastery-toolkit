# Research Library — Governance Document

**EIS Subdirectory:** 4_Research_Library
**CX Authority:** CX-EIS
**Created:** 2026-03-06
**Last Updated:** 2026-03-06

---

## Definition

**What belongs here:**
- Curated external research, studies, and reports relevant to [Your Organization]'s domain (AI education, solo practice business models, community building, adult learning)
- Frameworks discovered externally that have validated utility (e.g., Jobs to Be Done, Community-Led Growth, Blue Ocean Strategy)
- Framework comparisons: how competing approaches differ and what the evidence says about their effectiveness
- Research that calibrates AI/technology market conditions for the education and practitioner segment
- Sourced statistics and data points that support strategic decisions (with source and date captured)

**What does NOT belong here:**
- [Your Organization]'s own applied frameworks → 4_Context_Files (strategy and preferences)
- AI tool reviews or product comparisons not relevant to strategic calibration → 9_Support_Documents
- General reading or notes not tied to a calibration question → 10_Incoming_Material (pending processing)
- Success cases of specific practitioners → 3_Success_Models
- Market signals from real interactions → 1_Market_Intelligence

---

## Calibration Question

**"What does external research and validated frameworks tell us about the effectiveness of [Your Organization]'s approach and the conditions in which it succeeds?"**

Sub-questions:
- Is there evidence that [Your Organization]'s core methodology (AI literacy for 3C practitioners) is validated by research?
- What frameworks best explain community growth, solo practice economics, and adult learning?
- What does research say about the AI education market trajectory?

---

## Update Triggers

**Event-triggered:**
- When [User] reads or encounters a research piece, study, or framework with clear calibration relevance → Research_Index.md entry + topic-filed note
- When AI surfaces a research-level insight during a session → flagged for [User]'s review; added to Research_Index.md after validation

**Standing AI scan:** No — this is a curated library, not a signal feed. Quality over quantity.

**Periodic:**
- Quarterly: Review Research_Index.md for entries that are no longer current (superseded by newer research)

---

## Who Updates

**Human input required:**
- [User]'s judgment on whether a piece of research is relevant and credible enough to enter the library
- Source verification (AI cannot independently verify source credibility)

**AI-executable:**
- Synthesizing patterns across accumulated research entries on a given topic
- Suggesting research relevance when a topic comes up in session that matches a library entry
- Flagging potential research relevance during session (for [User]'s review before adding)

**Joint:**
- Framing how a research finding applies to [Your Organization]'s specific context

---

## Priority Intelligence Engine Connection

**Indirect.** Research Library provides background validation for strategic decisions, not daily task prioritization. The PIE does not read the Research Library in its standing Layer 2 synthesis.

**Triggered use:** When a major strategic decision is being made (e.g., redesigning the offer, choosing a new market segment), the relevant Research Library entries are read as part of the strategic evaluation context.

---

## Daily Brief Integration

**No.** The Research Library is a reference resource, not a signal feed.

---

## Step Function Connection

**Yes — indirectly.** A newly discovered framework or research finding can reveal:
- A validated growth mechanism [Your Organization] hasn't implemented → potential Step Function if the mechanism has non-linear return characteristics
- Research confirming a market inflection point → potential timing window (logged to 6_Timing_Externalities and flagged as Step Function candidate)
